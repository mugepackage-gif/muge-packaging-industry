Material and structure checks before quoting

  • Material direction: confirm whether the project should use PVC, PET or another clear material based on display, folding and target-market needs.
  • Product fit: provide exact product dimensions, weight, shape and packing orientation. Clear boxes show poor fit more obviously than paper boxes.
  • Crease and closure: review fold lines, locking tabs, bottom structure and opening direction before artwork is finalized.
  • Print or label area: define where branding sits, whether white ink is needed, and whether the clear area should remain unobstructed.
  • Retail and shipping path: confirm whether the package is for shelf display only, e-commerce shipping, gift sets or distributor packing.
  • Documentation boundary: do not assume transparent packaging is automatically suitable for every market or product use. Ask for project-specific material and document review.

Clear PVC box and PET clear box decision notes

Clear packaging works best when buyers need the product to be visible before opening. It is less forgiving than an opaque paper box because dust, scratches, loose fit, weak creases and poor insert planning can be seen immediately. Buyers comparing PVC packaging suppliers or a clear PET box manufacturer should ask whether the supplier can review samples or mockups against the actual product dimensions before confirming the final structure and artwork direction.

Supplier review note

A useful clear box supplier or clear PET box manufacturer should ask for product dimensions, retail channel, display direction, print artwork and packing method before providing a production quote. Buyers should be cautious with quotes that compare only unit price while skipping fit review, crease planning, sample review and export packing details.

Quote brief checklist

  • Product dimensions, product weight and photos or drawings.
  • Target quantity, SKU count and whether this is a pilot order or repeat order.
  • Preferred material direction: PVC, PET or supplier recommendation.
  • Branding method: printed logo, label, white ink, sticker, sleeve or paper insert.
  • Retail market and target sales channel: shelf, e-commerce, promotional set or gift pack.
  • Any buyer documentation requirement that must be reviewed before production.
